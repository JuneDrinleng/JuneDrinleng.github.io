## 1 When the length of a supramolecule changes, its emitted light changes accordingly

> Ref: A. Ryabchun *et al.*, *Angew Chem Int Ed*, e202507358 (2025).

The core mechanism is that the supramolecular has a structure like a drawstring. Its molecular conformation is ring-shaped at normal length. However, once external mechanical stress is applied and its length is shortened, its conformation changes and the hydrogen-bonded ring is broken, changing the wavelength of its emitted light.

## 2 Unit supramolecular plug-in composed of $\pi-\pi$ stacking

> Ref: Y. J. Lee *et al.*, *J. Am. Chem. Soc.* (2025), doi:[10.1021/jacs.5c03996](https://doi.org/10.1021/jacs.5c03996).

![952e62de432e0868e74412ea61dd373](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/952e62de432e0868e74412ea61dd373.png)

The blue molecular chain is a free group. The direct interaction of molecules mainly relies on the $\pi-\pi$ stacking between benzene rings. Moreover, due to the relationship between $sp^2$C and $sp^3$N, the two nearest biphenyl side chains are 90°, which can be used to form building blocks in two directions.

![6b0cfc970ec122e727535d60d58007f](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/6b0cfc970ec122e727535d60d58007f.png)

The electron microscope picture of the final supramolecular assembly looks like this, like layers of buildings stacked together.

![image-20250619101606423](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20250619101606423.png)

In addition, when the tail group is modified, biotin can be fluorescently labeled, and when benzoic acid is used to replace biphenyl, it can also interact with other positively charged structures for dyeing.

## 3 His modified Ferritin protein produces catalase-like catalytic activity

> Ref: J. Tian, B. Maity, T. Furuta, T. Pan, T. Ueno, *Angewandte Chemie International Edition*. **n/a**, e202504608.

![7fe33e85dd7a0932ebb104b579eaabe](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/7fe33e85dd7a0932ebb104b579eaabe.png)

Normally, Ferritin can form a 24-body self-assembly cage, but in this work, Ferritin was modified and mutated so that it has some His residues, thus forming a high-density acid-base/coordination site with His-related residues based on His-His interaction, which can catalyze the oxidation of other substrates by hydrogen peroxide.
