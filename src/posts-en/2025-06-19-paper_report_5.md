## 1 Emission Light Changes When Supermolecular Length Alters

> Ref: A. Ryabchun *et al.*, *Angew Chem Int Ed*, e202507358 (2025).

The core mechanism involves a rope-like structure in supermolecules, where the molecular conformation is ring-shaped at normal length. However, when external mechanical stress is applied, the length shortens, altering the conformation and breaking the hydrogen-bonding ring, which changes the emission wavelength of light

## 2 $\pi-\pi$ Stacking-Formed Unit Supermolecular Plugin

> Ref: Y. J. Lee *et al.*, *J. Am. Chem. Soc.* (2025), doi:[10.1021/jacs.5c03996](https://doi.org/10.1021/jacs.5c03996).

![952e62de432e0868e74412ea61dd373](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/952e62de432e0868e74412ea61dd373.png)

The blue molecular chain is a free group, with intermolecular interactions primarily relying on $\pi-\pi$ stacking between benzene rings. Due to the relationship between $sp^2$C and $sp^3$N, the two adjacent biphenyl side chains form a 90° angle, enabling the formation of building blocks in two directions

![6b0cfc970ec122e727535d60d58007f](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/6b0cfc970ec122e727535d60d58007f.png)

The final electron microscope image of the supermolecular assembly looks like this, resembling layers of buildings stacked together

![image-20250619101606423](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20250619101606423.png)

Additionally, modifying the terminal groups with biotin allows fluorescent labeling. Replacing the biphenyl with benzoic acid enables interaction with positively charged structures for staining

## 3 His-Modified Ferritin Protein Generates Catalytic Activity Similar to Catalase

> Ref: J. Tian, B. Maity, T. Furuta, T. Pan, T. Ueno, *Angewandte Chemie International Edition*. **n/a**, e202504608.

![7fe33e85dd7a0932ebb104b579eaabe](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/7fe33e85dd7a0932ebb104b579eaabe.png)

Normally, Ferritin can form a 24-meric self-assembled cage. However, this work modifies and mutates Ferritin to introduce His residues, enabling His-His interactions to create high-density acid-base/coordination sites with His-related residues, allowing catalytic oxidation of hydrogen peroxide and other substrates
