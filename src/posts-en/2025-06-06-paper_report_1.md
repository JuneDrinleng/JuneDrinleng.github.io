## 1 Heterogeneous interface interlocking hydrogels utilizing cellulose scaffolds for robust and controllable soft actuators

> reference: [Y. Song *et al.*, *Advanced Functional Materials*. **n/a**, 2509712 (2025).](https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/adfm.202509712)

![65fda325e47fe2bcd9ad57e4cb31a22](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/65fda325e47fe2bcd9ad57e4cb31a22.png)

The material is inspired by the leaf structure. The entire hydrogel is polymerized in situ on the cellulose skeleton and exhibits its electrical conductivity, stress-strain curve, damping effect and other mechanical properties.

![de58c3715bbada03ca9617e81544ed9](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/de58c3715bbada03ca9617e81544ed9.png)

The material can be hardened from a softer part and its tensile strain properties are tested

![93d435a544432b24ffad378121f32eb](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/93d435a544432b24ffad378121f32eb.png)

Soaking in different concentrations of ferric ions gradually produces a bending effect (related to the salting out effect. Under different concentrations of $Fe^{3+}$ environments, the water content inside the hydrogel is different). At the same time, it shows a dynamic dissociation process in hydrochloric acid solution.

![949ff607dfada406fc2c293afe78dd1](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/949ff607dfada406fc2c293afe78dd1.png)

The selection of different particles will also affect the bending angle of the hydrogel material. At the same time, compared with the control group, the PBCI material maintains its bending deformation better after de-crosslinking and re-crosslinking, and the material has better stability to pressure and cycles (Figure g, h)
