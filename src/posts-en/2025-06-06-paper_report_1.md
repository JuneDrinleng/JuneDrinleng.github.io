## 1 Utilizing Cellulose Scaffold to Achieve a Robust and Controllable Soft Actuator with a Heterogeneous Interface Interlocked Hydrogel

> reference: [Y. Song *et al.*, *Advanced Functional Materials*. **n/a**, 2509712 (2025).](https://advanced.onlinelibrary.wiley.com/doi/10.1002/adfm.202509712)

![65fda325e47fe2bcd9ad57e4cb31a22](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/65fda325e47fe2bcd9ad57e4cb31a22.png)

The material is inspired by leaf structures, with the entire hydrogel undergoing in-situ polymerization on the cellulose scaffold, exhibiting conductivity, stress-strain curves, damping effects, and other mechanical properties

![de58c3715bbada03ca9617e81544ed9](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/de58c3715bbada03ca9617e81544ed9.png)

The material can transition from a relatively soft state to hardening, and its tensile strain performance was tested

![93d435a544432b24ffad378121f32eb](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/93d435a544432b24ffad378121f32eb.png)

Immersion in different concentrations of trivalent iron ions leads to gradual bending (related to salting-out effects, with different $Fe^{3+}$ environments causing varying water content in the hydrogel), while in hydrochloric acid solutions, it exhibits dynamic dissociation

![949ff607dfada406fc2c293afe78dd1](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/949ff607dfada406fc2c293afe78dd1.png)

Using different particles also affects the bending angle of the hydrogel material. Compared to the control group, PBCI material maintains better bending deformation after de-crosslinking and re-crosslinking, showing good stability against pressure and cycling (Figures g,h)
