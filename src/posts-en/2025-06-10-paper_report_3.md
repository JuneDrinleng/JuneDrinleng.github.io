## 1 MOF modifies the surface of cell-like droplets to explore the interaction between primitive cell-like droplets

> Ref: [ Y. Ji, Y. Lin, Y. Qiao, Nat. Chem., 1–11 (2025)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41557-025-01827-7)

There are still challenges in using membrane-free droplets to simulate cell tissue and interactions between cells. The author's team has developed a way to modify the surface of membrane-less droplets based on MOF materials. With the help of MOF, it can realize the function of membrane-embedded proteins in cells, thereby being able to mimic the proteins of the entire cell membrane and intramembrane proteins.

![img](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41557_2025_1827_Figa_HTML.png)

The interaction between the multiple cell-like droplets at the end is beautiful!

![Fig. 1](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41557_2025_1827_Fig1_HTML.png)

For cells, there are some embedded cells inside the cell membrane, and there are also some proteins on the inner surface of the cell membrane (Peripheral membrane protein). These cells build tissues through a number of interactions and connections.

So for the primitive cell-like droplets in this article, it is also a primitive cell-like droplet. MOF materials embedded with proteins are added, and these MOF materials are wrapped on the surface of the primitive cell-like droplets to form a surface-modified primitive cell-like droplet. Finally, primitive tissue-like tissue is formed through aggregation.

In general, it is equivalent to using this protocell to simulate the cell functions in the above picture a.

For MOF, there are two types, one is to embed GOx and HRP inside the MOF, and the other is to adsorb these proteins on the surface of the MOF.

![Fig. 2](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41557_2025_1827_Fig2_HTML.png)

Here, a series of characterizations of MOF-modified primitive cells were made, including surface tension (the surface tension is obviously reduced after modification), core-shell structure distribution (spatial distribution, radial scanning, and cross-sectional scanning). In addition, the fluidity of droplets (FRAP) and the obvious droplet growth ability of modified MOF compared to unmodified MOF were measured (FSCA-A, Time).

Figure j begins to explore the compatibility of modified and unmodified. It can be found that the unmodified and the unmodified are compatible, the modified and the modified are not compatible, and the unmodified and the modified are incompatible with each other (i-p, m-p are evidence)

![Fig. 3](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41557_2025_1827_Fig3_HTML.png)

What is explored here is the effect of doping molecules with different molecular weights/charges inside the droplets on the droplets. For the same large charge, those with larger molecular weights will tend to be fully embedded on the MOF membrane, while smaller ones will not. The red ball (f) with small charge and molecular weight is partially embedded. Verification via radial fluorescence intensity

![Fig. 4](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41557_2025_1827_Fig4_HTML.png)

The wonderful thing here is that through such an HRP catalyst, amplex red (a precursor) can be reflected into resorufin (red), making the droplets gradually turn red, while excess hydrogen peroxide will oxidize the red resorufin, making the red light gradually dim.

Then based on the above, the above results can be used to construct artificial bionic primitive cells:
![Fig. 5](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41557_2025_1827_Fig5_HTML.png)

The most beautiful thing is that the team then used this bionic cell to study interactions between multiple cells:

![Fig. 6](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41557_2025_1827_Fig6_HTML.png)

You can see the sobriety of the original tissue in picture b. In addition, changing the linking strategy of the protocell can form different tissue shapes.

After forming a tissue, it can even form a multi-cell coordinated reaction pathway (f-g)

## 2 Tunable rapid assembly of collagen elements

> Ref: [X. Gong *et al.*, *Nat. Mater.*, 1–12 (2025).](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-025-02241-7)

From the beginning of this article, it seems to be a normal trend, until the last picture, it rises in place.

Challenges exist in the assembly and adhesion of collagen in vitro, which limits its use as biological scaffolds and adhesives. The author's team proposed a collagen manufacturing method - macromolecule extrusion and timely assembly, which can achieve controllable bioprinting and can be widely used to directly print cell tissues and even organoids.

![Fig. 1](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41563_2025_2241_Fig1_HTML.png)

Compared with direct transaction of collagen, gel formation is rapid after adding PEG8k. The transparency of Figure c also reflects the degree of cross-linking to a certain extent (the higher the degree of cross-linking, the higher the transparency)

During preparation, it is absorbed by beating and then sprayed out - evolved into the inkjet printing method, which only needs to make collagen and PEG come into contact, and it can be used in patterning (such as o)

![Fig. 2](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41563_2025_2241_Fig2_HTML.png)

Based on this, some microvessels can be formed, and these microvessels will continue to grow.

And compared with the regular gel formation method, the microvessels obtained by this kind of extrusion are uniform and will not cause collagen to settle due to gravity:

![Fig. 3](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41563_2025_2241_Fig3_HTML.png)

Not only can it be patterned, it can also be spun:
![Fig. 4](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41563_2025_2241_Fig4_HTML.png)

The last picture is awesome, he directly printed a heart organoid:
![Fig. 5](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41563_2025_2241_Fig5_HTML.png)
