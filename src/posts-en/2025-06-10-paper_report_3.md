## 1 MOF-Modified Protocell Droplets: Investigating Interactions Between Protocell Droplets

> Ref: [ Y. Ji, Y. Lin, Y. Qiao, *Nat. Chem.*, 1–11 (2025)](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41557-025-01827-7)

Simulating cellular organization and interactions between cells using membraneless droplets remains challenging. The authors developed a method to modify the surface of membraneless droplets using MOF materials, enabling the incorporation of membrane-embedded proteins in cells to mimic the entire cell membrane and intracellular proteins

![img](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41557_2025_1827_Figa_HTML.png)

The interaction between multiple protocell droplets is beautifully demonstrated!

![Fig. 1](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41557_2025_1827_Fig1_HTML.png)

For cells, there are embedded proteins within the cell membrane and peripheral membrane proteins on the inner surface of the membrane. These cells form tissues through interactions and linkages.

For the protocells in this paper, they are also protocell droplets. By incorporating MOF materials with embedded proteins, these MOF materials are wrapped around the surface of protocell droplets, forming surface-modified protocells, which then aggregate to form protocellular tissues.

In summary, this approach uses protocells to simulate the functions depicted in Figure a.

For MOF, there are two types: one with GOx and HRP embedded in the MOF interior, and another with these proteins adsorbed on the MOF surface

![Fig. 2](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41557_2025_1827_Fig2_HTML.png)

The paper conducted a series of characterizations on MOF-modified protocells, including surface tension (which decreased significantly after modification), nuclear-shell structure distribution (spatial distribution and radial scanning, cross-sectional scanning), and additionally measured droplet mobility (FRAP). The modified MOF showed significantly enhanced droplet growth capability compared to unmodified MOF (FSCA-A, Time).

Figure j investigates the compatibility between modified and unmodified droplets. It was found that unmodified droplets can coexist with unmodified ones, but modified droplets cannot coalesce with modified ones. Unmodified and modified droplets are incompatible (i-p, m-p are evidence).

![Fig. 3](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41557_2025_1827_Fig3_HTML.png)

This section explores the impact of different molecular weight/charge molecules inside droplets on droplet behavior. For molecules with the same charge, larger molecular weight ones tend to embed entirely in the MOF membrane, while smaller ones do not. Molecules with small molecular weight and charge (red ball f) partially embed. This is validated through radial fluorescence intensity measurements.

![Fig. 4](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41557_2025_1827_Fig4_HTML.png)

The elegance here lies in using HRP as a catalyst to convert amplex red (a precursor) into resorufin (red), causing droplets to gradually turn red. Excess hydrogen peroxide oxidizes the red resorufin, making the red light fade.

Based on these results, artificial protocells mimicking protocellular functions can be constructed:
![Fig. 5](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41557_2025_1827_Fig5_HTML.png)

Most impressively, the team further used these protocells to study interactions between multiple cells:

![Fig. 6](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41557_2025_1827_Fig6_HTML.png)

The image shows the formation of protocellular tissues as depicted in Figure b. Changing the linking strategy of protocells can form different tissue shapes.

After forming tissues, they can even establish multi-cellular collaborative reaction pathways (f-g)

## 2 Tunable Rapid Assembly of Collagen Protein Elements
> Ref:  [X. Gong *et al.*, *Nat. Mater.*, 1–12 (2025).](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41563-025-02241-7)

The paper appears to follow a normal trajectory at first, but the last figure dramatically elevates the prestige.

Collagen assembly and adhesion in vitro face challenges that limit its application as a biological scaffold and adhesive. The authors propose a collagen manufacturing method—large molecule extrusion and timely assembly—which enables controllable bioprinting and can be widely applied for direct printing of cell tissues and organoids.

![Fig. 1](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41563_2025_2241_Fig1_HTML.png)

Compared to direct collagen crosslinking, adding PEG8k enables rapid gelation. The transparency in Figure c also reflects crosslinking degree (higher crosslinking degree corresponds to higher transparency).

During preparation, the method evolved from a pounding and suction extrusion technique to inkjet printing, requiring only contact between collagen and PEG for patterned applications (e.g., o).

![Fig. 2](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41563_2025_2241_Fig2_HTML.png)

Based on this, microvascular networks can be formed, which continue to grow.

Compared to regular gelation methods, the extrusion-derived microvessels are uniform and avoid collagen sedimentation caused by gravity:

![Fig. 3](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41563_2025_2241_Fig3_HTML.png)

The method enables both patterned fabrication and fiber spinning:
![Fig. 4](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41563_2025_2241_Fig4_HTML.png)

The final figure is impressive, as it directly prints a cardiac organoid:
![Fig. 5](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/41563_2025_2241_Fig5_HTML.png)

Rhythmic contraction and relaxation were observed.
