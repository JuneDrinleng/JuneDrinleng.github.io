# 2 Basics in with Probability
Frequency probability:

$$
P(x) \approx \frac{n_x}{n_t}
$$
$$
P(x)=lim\frac{n_x}{n_t}
$$

Bayesian Probability:

![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20240909102407997.png)


X is the cause Y is the result (observation), what is needed now is to deduce the cause from the result

So from:

$$
P(XY)=P(X|Y)P(Y)=P(Y|X)P(X)
$$

After deformation:

$$
P(X|Y)=\frac{P(Y|X)P(X)}{P(Y)}
$$

And:

$$
P(Y)=\Sigma_XP(Y|X)P(X)
$$

Independence reduces freedom of reasoning

If X and Y are independent then:

$$
P(XY)=P(X)P(Y)
$$

# 3 Basics in Graph
The classic origin of graph theory is the seven-bridge problem of building bridges.

A general directed graph is represented by G, V represents the node, and E represents the edge:

$$G(V,E)$$

An undirected graph is represented by H (the same path is reachable in both directions)

Critical matrix:

$$A={a_{ij}}_{n*n}$$

![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20240909112152821.png)



Polytree means that a single node can have multiple parent nodes but cannot have triangles or quadrilaterals.

A chordal graph is a polygon whose legend cannot have more than triangles.

# Three Steps for PGMs
1. Representation: how to model your problems using probability and graph——modeling
2. Inference: how to calculate the posterior with some given/observed variables (model is known)
3. Learning: how to estimate the parameters of the model when you only have observed data
