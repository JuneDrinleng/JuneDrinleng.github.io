# 1 Introduction

The course mainly uses graph models to solve complex probabilistic problems (excluding coding-related content)

Probability is a mathematical model used to describe uncertainty

# 2 Basics in Probability

Frequency probability:

$$
P(x) \approx \frac{n_x}{n_t}
$$

$$
P(x)=lim\frac{n_x}{n_t}
$$

Bayesian probability:

![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20240909102407997.png)

X is the cause, Y is the result (observation), and now we need to infer the cause from the result

Thus, from:

$$
P(XY)=P(X|Y)P(Y)=P(Y|X)P(X)
$$

After transformation:

$$
P(X|Y)=\frac{P(Y|X)P(X)}{P(Y)}
$$

And:

$$
P(Y)=\Sigma_XP(Y|X)P(X)
$$

Independence reduces the degrees of freedom in inference

If X and Y are independent, then:

$$
P(XY)=P(X)P(Y)
$$

Most commonly used formula:

Chain rule:

# 3 Basics in Graph

The classical origin of graph theory is the Seven Bridges of Königsberg problem

A general directed graph is denoted by G, with V representing nodes and E representing edges:

$$ G(V,E) $$

An undirected graph is denoted by H (bidirectional reachability on the same path)

Adjacency matrix:

$$A={a_{ij}}_{n*n}$$

![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20240909112152821.png)

Polytree is a graph where a single node can have multiple parent nodes but cannot have triangles or quadrilaterals

Chordal graph is a graph where no polygon with more than a triangle exists

# 4 Probabilistic Graphical Models (PGMs)

Advantages of PGMs in representation:

Compared to directly looking at formulas, viewing graphs provides a clearer understanding of relationships between events

Provides a uniform framework for reasoning

In an undirected graph, the connection between the four white nodes above is bidirectional  
![](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20240909113531985.png)

Undirected graphs are widely used in computer vision, NLP, and speech recognition. For example, Markov Random Fields are widely applied in image segmentation

# Three Steps for PGMs

1. Representation: How to model problems using probability and graph - i.e., modeling
2. Inference: How to calculate the posterior with some given/observed variables (model is known)
3. Learning: How to estimate the parameters of the model when you only have observed data
