## 1 Multi-chamber hybrid microreactor for enzyme catalysis

> ref: X. Hao *et al.*, *Angew Chem Int Ed*, e202502479 (2025).

![image-20250902171358555](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20250902171358555.png)

The strategy of this article is to form agglomerates similar to precipitation through the mutual attraction of charges of anionic and cationic polymers, and then add silica emulsifier to wrap this group of agglomerates to form droplets. The droplets are shown in Figures b and c. It is close to the previous LPS experiment, and the signal is mainly concentrated at the boundary (it may also be because of the outer silicon oxide shell).  

After constructing the above-mentioned microreactor, the literature introduced different molecular reaction environments, such as CLAB, GOx, HRP, NADH, etc., characterized its spatiotemporal heterogeneity, and then demonstrated its reaction selectivity:

## 2 Ballistic diffusion in biomolecular condensates

> ref: W. Chen *et al.*, *Nat. Nanotechnol.*, 1–9 (2025).

The formation of the entire droplet is like this. Below is the copolymerization of $A_{20}/T_{20}$ to form a droplet with a blue DNA segment on the outside and a green DNA segment on the inside. Then first add the dye 647 that binds to the blue DNA segments, then the entire droplet boundary is marked by the 647 dye, and then add the green 488 dye. You can find that the green color spreads radially and centripetally from the circumference to the center of the circle, until all the green DNA segments inside the entire droplet are combined with the 488 dye and become green fluorescence.

![image-20250902173211689](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20250902173211689.png)

The fickian diffusion here is the macroscopic statistical result of Brownian motion, and the ballistic wave diffusion here is what we understand:
$$
MSD=2D t^2
$$
Its diffusion index $\alpha=2$, and its diffusion performance macroscopically is as shown in the figure below

![image-20250902175547637](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20250902175547637.png)
