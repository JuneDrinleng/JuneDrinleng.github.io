## 1 Multi-Compartment Microreactor for Enzyme Catalysis

> ref: X. Hao *et al.*, *Angew Chem Int Ed*, e202502479 (2025).

![image-20250902171358555](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20250902171358555.png)

The strategy of this paper is to form aggregate-like precipitates through electrostatic attraction between cation-anion polymers, then encapsulate these aggregates with silica emulsifier to form droplets, as shown in figures b and c. Its experimental setup is similar to previous LLPS experiments, with signals mainly aggregated at the boundaries (possibly due to the silica oxide shell on the exterior).  

After constructing the aforementioned microreactor, the paper introduces different molecular reaction environments, such as CLAB, GOx, HRP, NADH, etc., characterizing their spatiotemporal heterogeneity and demonstrating reaction selectivity:

## 2 Ballistic Diffusion in Biomolecular Condensates

> ref: W. Chen *et al.*, *Nat. Nanotechnol.*, 1–9 (2025).

The formation of droplets is as follows: $A_{20}/T_{20}$ copolymerization creates droplets with blue DNA segments on the exterior and green DNA segments internally. First, a dye 647 conjugated to blue DNA segments is added, marking the droplet boundary with 647 dye. Subsequently, green dye 488 is added, revealing radial inward diffusion of green fluorescence from the perimeter to the center until all green DNA segments within the droplet bind to 488 dye, resulting in green fluorescence.

![image-20250902173211689](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20250902173211689.png)

Here, Fickian diffusion represents the macroscopic statistical result of Brownian motion, while ballistic wave diffusion refers to:
$$
MSD=2D t^2
$$
with a diffusion exponent $\alpha=2$. The macroscopic manifestation of this diffusion is shown in the following figure:

![image-20250902175547637](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20250902175547637.png)
