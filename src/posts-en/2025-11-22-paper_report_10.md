## 1 Flexible Tetrahelical Helix Shows Strong Binding Preference for DNA Triplet via Induced Fit

> H. D. Williams, S. J. Dettmer, S. Bajpai, M. J. Hannon, *J. Am. Chem. Soc.* **147**, 40992–41003 (2025).

The $[Pt_2L_4]^{4+}$ complex formed with 3-way DNA (3WJ) exhibits strong binding preference for the DNA triplet, but when 4-way DNA (4WJ) is used, although the shape of the DNA quadruplet matches that of the $[Pt_2L_4]^{4+}$ polymer, the binding energy is lower than that of the triplet match.

![image-20251122102112229](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20251122102112229.png)

It is speculated that the reason might be that although the shapes are similar, the cavity of the quadruplet is much larger than the core, making the base-core π-electron interactions not necessarily reliable; whereas the triplet, although the cavity is small and the shape does not match, the size matches, allowing the triplet cavity and core to be close enough for π-π interactions, leading to a large binding energy.

![image-20251122103344833](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20251122103344833.png)

This figure shows the specific structure and recognition schematic, see C. The L is the ligand on the right side of C, coordinating with Pt via pyridine-N and phenol-O groups. The specific coordination mode is shown in the left figure of C, with blue representing N and red representing O, mainly coordinated by N.

![image-20251122103902400](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20251122103902400.png)

To fit into the DNA triplet, the core actually pushes apart some base pairs of the DNA triplet, as shown in the pink area of Figure A. After entering, there are numerous interactions between the DNA triplet and the core, such as the pyridine ring of Pt ligands and the adjacent base stacking effect (1:1 stacking); the aromatic ring of base pairs and the pyridine ring of Pt ligands with multiple stacking base pair stacking effects. In addition, there are transient interactions, such as transient π-stacking and transient π-CH interactions.

Here, a lower $k_d$ indicates stronger binding and greater stability. The authors explored the difference between platinum and nickel, finding that nickel binds more easily than platinum (due to nickel's smaller radius, and thus a smaller core, resulting in less disruption of DNA).

## 2 Conformation and Dynamics of Single, Phase-Separated, and Cross-Linked Insect Elastin Biomaterials

![image-20251122114706343](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20251122114706343.png)

The main focus is on analyzing the conformational changes of resilin protein (**elastin LLPS**): the conformation, dynamics, and intermolecular interactions of D3 as a monomer, in phase-separated state, and as a cross-linked gel. In elucidating the mechanism of D3 LLPS, a complex set of electrostatic and π-π interactions, along with hydrophobic effects, were found to fine-tune the solution sensitivity of D3 and its capacity for LLPS.

![image-20251122115458779](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20251122115458779.png)

Other content involves NMR to study the conformation.
