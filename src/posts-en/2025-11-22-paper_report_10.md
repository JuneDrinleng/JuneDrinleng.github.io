## 1 Flexible four-stranded helix shows strong binding preference for DNA triplet through induced fit

> H. D. Williams, S. J. Dettmer, S. Bajpai, M. J. Hannon, *J. Am. Chem. Soc.* **147**, 40992–41003 (2025).

$[Pt_2L_4]^{4+}$ composed of platinum and ligand L can be added to 3-way DNA (3WJ) to form the conjugate on the right, which shows the binding preference of this quadruple helix for DNA triplets. However, if 4-way DNA (4WJ) is added, although the shape of the DNA quadruplex is the same as $[Pt_2L_4]^{4+}$ The shapes of the polymers match, but their binding energies are smaller than those of the triplet.

![image-20251122102112229](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20251122102112229.png)

It is speculated that although the shapes are similar, the cavity of the quadruplex is much larger than the core, so that the base-core π electron interaction is not necessarily reliable; while the triplet has a small cavity and does not match the shape, but the size does match. The close size of the triplet cavity and core makes the π-π interaction close enough, resulting in a large binding energy.

![image-20251122103344833](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20251122103344833.png)

This figure is the specific structure and identification diagram, see Figure C. L is the ligand on the right side of Figure C. It coordinates Pt through pyridine-N and phenol-O groups. The specific coordination method is as shown in the left figure of Figure C. Blue is N, red O, mainly N is used for coordination.

![image-20251122103902400](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20251122103902400.png)

In order to squeeze into the DNA triplet, the core actually pushes apart some of the base pairs of the DNA triplet, as shown in the pink part in Figure A. After entering, there are many interactions between the DNA triplet and the core, such as the Base Stacking effect of the pyridine ring of the Pt ligand and the adjacent bases (one-to-one stacking); the one-to-many stacking Base Pair stacking effect of the base pairs of the aromatic ring and the pyridine ring of the Pt ligand. In addition, there are also the effects of Transient, some instantaneous effects, instantaneous π stacking and instantaneous π-CH interaction.

The lower the kd here, the easier it is to combine and the more stable the combination. Here the author explores the difference between platinum and nickel. Nickel is easier to combine than platinum (because the radius of nickel is small, and of course the core is relatively smaller, so it does not damage DNA as much)

## 2 Conformation and dynamics of monomeric, phase-separated and cross-linked arthroelastin biomaterials

![image-20251122114706343](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20251122114706343.png)

The main purpose is to analyze the conformational changes of this resilin protein (**elastin LLPS**): the conformation, dynamics and intermolecular interactions of D3 as a monomer, in a phase separation state and as a cross-linked gel were studied. In elucidating the mechanism of action of D3 LLPS, we discovered a complex set of electrostatic and π-based interactions, complemented by hydrophobic effects, that fine-tune the solution sensitivity of D3 and its capacity for LLPS

![image-20251122115458779](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/JuneDrinleng/JuneDrinleng.github.io/main/assets/img/image-20251122115458779.png)

The rest of the content is based on NMR analysis to see the conformation.
